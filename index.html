Battery Thermal Management for Li-ion Batteries in Simulated Environment

This MSc thesis focused on improving the safety and performance of lithium-ion batteries by investigating advanced thermal management strategies. Through a combination of laboratory experiments and MATLAB-based modelling, three cooling approaches—air, indirect liquid, and dielectric-fluid immersion—were compared using prismatic LiFePO4 cells. The research quantified heat generation, extracted entropic heat coefficients, and validated a real-time capable lumped thermal model suitable for Model-in-the-Loop and Hardware-in-the-Loop testing. Results showed that immersion cooling achieved the most effective temperature control and uniformity, offering valuable insights for the design of safer, more efficient electric vehicles and stationary storage systems.

Estimated Entropic Heat Coefficient range: -0.29 to 0.24 mV/K View Details

RadiAir: Saving Space and Winning Back Time in Modern Urban Living

This team assignment (presented in Reserch & Innovation Showcase, 2025 hosted by Trinity College Dublin) designed a novel heat exchanger that combines central heating with clothes drying capabilities through natural convection. It delivered 1,000W for ambient heating and up to 2,000W in drying mode, using simple geometries like multiport tubing for easy retrofitting and no maintenance. Performance predictions and market assessments indicated potential for 270,000 households in Ireland, with expansion to global markets in North America, Asia, and Europe.

CryoSync: Pushing Performance Limits - A Next-Gen Thermal Management Solutions for High-Power GPUs

This collaborative project engineered a liquid cooling solution with jet impingement arrays and dynamic pump control to manage thermal loads in high-power GPUs. The aluminum construction and modular top plate enabled low temperatures for overclocking, energy efficiency, and customization. Analytical estimates and CFD results demonstrated superior thermal performance, aligning with trends in the gaming PC market for enhanced reliability and personalization.

Achieved dT @ 2.5°C with 85.8% Pump Power Reduction View Details

VaxVac: Keeping Vaccines Safe, Anywhere

This group project at Trinity College Dublin developed a vacuum-based refrigeration system to address vaccine wastage in high-temperature settings. The design incorporated an aluminum shell, UF foam insulation, and a hand-pump mechanism to maintain temperatures between 2°C and 8°C for over 10 hours. Thermal resistance networks and CFD simulations confirmed uniform temperature distribution and compliance with storage standards, supporting use by philanthropic organizations in remote areas.

Maintained storage @ 2°C-8°C for 10+ hours View Details

Vehicle Optimization for Reduced Emissions

This assignment modeled a 2014 Mazda 3 Skyactiv vehicle to optimize fuel consumption and CO2 emissions over the Modified Indian Driving Cycle. Techniques included mass and drag reduction, gear shifting strategy refinement, gear ratio optimization, and ethanol-blended gasoline use. Results showed up to 12.8% emission reduction but milage dropped by 7.86% with E20 Blend, with significant fleet-wide savings estimated for C-class sedans.

Cut emissions by 12.8% with E20 Blend. View Details

Offshore Wind Energy Resource Assessment

This MSc project at Trinity College Dublin analyzed offshore wind potential in Ireland using data from marine weather buoys (study was done for M2 and M4, but script is flexible enough to be used for any weather bouys with correct formatting). It involved statistical analysis of wind speed, probability distribution functions (Weibull and Rayleigh), power spectral density, wind speed extrapolation to 100m using log and power laws, and turbine suitability assessment with the Siemens-Gamesa SG 10.0-193 DD. The study highlighted M4's superior wind characteristics and included environmental and social considerations.

Identified site for 1.5GW offshore wind target. View Details
